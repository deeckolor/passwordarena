# Module 2 — Password Arena

Password Strength & Crack-Time Simulator
Most people know their passwords could be stronger. Almost nobody knows exactly how weak they really are — or why the tricks they rely on stopped working years ago.
Password Arena shows you the truth in real time.
Type any password and watch a live analyzer calculate exactly how long a modern GPU cracking cluster — running ten billion guesses per second — would take to break it. The results are often shocking. "P@ssw0rd" falls in under a second. "iloveyou2026" fares little better. Even passwords that feel complex crumble against dictionary attacks and leet-speak rule sets that crackers have used for decades.
The module walks you through five progressive challenges designed to build genuine password intuition. You'll start with basic complexity requirements and work your way up to entropy mastery — learning why length matters more than any other single factor, why three random words separated by spaces outperforms a symbol-stuffed eight-character password, and why the tricks you were taught in 2010 are now well-known to every password-cracking tool in existence.
Every keystroke updates the entropy calculation, the crack-time estimate, the charset analysis, and a live list of your password's specific weaknesses and strengths. There are no multiple choice questions here — just you, a password field, and the mathematics of security.

## What you'll walk away with

A clear mental model of what makes passwords actually strong, a practical passphrase technique you can use immediately, and a healthy distrust of password advice that was outdated before you heard it.

## CyberSafe for Girls in ICT Day

CyberSafe was developed to support the ITU's Girls in ICT Day mission of empowering women and girls to pursue careers in technology — and to ensure that every woman who enters the digital world does so equipped to protect herself in it.
